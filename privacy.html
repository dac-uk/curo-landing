Privacy Policy

Bamboo Futures Ltd · Last updated: 29 April 2026 · Effective: 29 April 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Bamboo Futures Ltd ("we", "us", "our") collects, uses, and protects your personal data when you use the Curo application and website. Please read it carefully. By using Curo, you confirm that you have read and understood this policy.

1. Who We Are

Bamboo Futures Ltd is the data controller responsible for your personal data. We are a company registered in England and Wales and registered with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO).

Contact details:
Bamboo Futures Ltd
Email: hello@bamboofutures.co.uk

If you have any questions about how we handle your data or wish to exercise any of your rights, please contact us at the address above.

2. What Data We Collect

2.1 Account data

When you create a Curo account we collect:

  • Your email address (used for authentication and service communications)
  • A hashed password (we never store your password in plain text)
  • The date and time of account creation

2.2 Open Banking / financial data

With your explicit consent, Curo connects to your bank accounts via FCA-regulated Open Banking providers. We collect and process:

  • Bank account metadata (account name, account number last four digits, sort code, account type, institution name)
  • Transaction data (amount, merchant name, date, category, transaction description, balance)

This access is strictly read-only. Curo cannot initiate payments, move money, or make any changes to your bank accounts whatsoever.

2.3 Usage analytics

We collect anonymised usage data to understand how the app is used and to improve the service. This includes:

  • Features accessed and frequency of use
  • App version and operating system version
  • Crash reports and error logs
  • Session duration and navigation patterns

We do not use third-party advertising analytics. All analytics data is processed in aggregate and is not linked to your identity.

2.4 Data we do NOT collect

We do not collect:

  • Your full bank account number or sort code
  • Your banking passwords or login credentials
  • Your payment card details
  • Location data
  • Any biometric data

3. How We Use Your Data

We use your personal data for the following purposes:

  • To provide the Curo service — powering your Morning Briefings, bill tracking, anomaly detection, and natural language queries
  • To manage your account — authentication, account recovery, and service notifications
  • To improve Curo — using aggregated analytics to understand usage patterns and prioritise product development
  • To comply with legal obligations — where we are required to retain or disclose data by law
  • To communicate with you — responding to support requests and, where you have consented, sending product updates

4. Legal Basis for Processing

Under UK GDPR, we rely on the following legal bases:

  • Contract performance (Article 6(1)(b)) — processing necessary to deliver the Curo service you have subscribed to, including all financial data analysis
  • Legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f)) — processing for the purposes of improving the service, preventing fraud, and maintaining security. We have carried out a Legitimate Interests Assessment and are satisfied these interests are not overridden by your rights
  • Consent (Article 6(1)(a)) — where we send you optional marketing or product update communications. You may withdraw consent at any time
  • Legal obligation (Article 6(1)(c)) — where we are required to process your data by applicable law

5. Open Banking and Third-Party Providers

To connect your bank accounts, Curo integrates with FCA-regulated Account Information Service Providers (AISPs). These providers establish a secure, consented connection to your bank on your behalf under the Payment Services Regulations 2017 and Open Banking standards.

Key points about Open Banking access:

  • You explicitly authorise each bank connection in the Curo app
  • Access is read-only at all times — we can never move or modify your money
  • Bank connections can be revoked by you at any time, within Curo or directly with your bank
  • Our Open Banking providers are authorised by the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) and operate under strict data protection obligations
  • Connections expire and must be re-authorised periodically (typically every 90 days) in accordance with Open Banking regulations

6. How We Protect Your Data

We take data security seriously and implement appropriate technical and organisational measures, including:

  • Encryption in transit — all data transmitted between your device and our servers is encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher
  • Encryption at rest — all financial data stored on our servers is encrypted at rest using AES-256
  • Access controls — strict role-based access controls ensure only authorised personnel can access personal data, and only for legitimate operational purposes
  • Minimal data retention — we only retain data for as long as necessary (see Section 7)
  • Regular security reviews — we conduct periodic security assessments of our infrastructure and practices

7. How Long We Keep Your Data

  • Transaction data — retained for 24 months from the transaction date, then automatically and permanently deleted
  • Account data — retained for the duration of your account and deleted within 30 days of account cancellation or deletion
  • Analytics data — retained in anonymised, aggregated form indefinitely; no individual records are retained beyond 12 months
  • Support correspondence — retained for 2 years after the matter is resolved, then deleted
  • Legal hold — where we are required by law to retain data for longer, we will do so only for the required period

8. Who We Share Your Data With

We do not sell your data. We do not share your data with advertisers. Ever.

We may share your data only in the following limited circumstances:

  • Open Banking providers — our FCA-regulated AISP partners, solely for the purpose of establishing bank connections
  • Cloud infrastructure providers — we use reputable cloud service providers to host our infrastructure. These providers act as data processors under contracts that include appropriate data protection clauses
  • Legal requirements — where we are legally compelled to disclose data, for example under a court order or to comply with a legal obligation. We will notify you of any such disclosure where legally permitted to do so
  • Business transfers — in the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or substantially all of our business, your data may be transferred to the acquirer. We will notify you in advance and you will have the right to delete your account before any such transfer

9. International Data Transfers

We store and process your data within the United Kingdom and the European Economic Area (EEA). Where any data is transferred outside the UK or EEA (for example, to cloud infrastructure in other regions), we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place in accordance with UK GDPR Chapter V, such as Standard Contractual Clauses or equivalent mechanisms.

10. Your Rights Under UK GDPR

You have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access (Article 15) — you may request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Right to rectification (Article 16) — you may request that we correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Right to erasure (Article 17) — you may request that we delete your personal data ("right to be forgotten"), subject to our legal obligations
  • Right to portability (Article 20) — you may request your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Right to restriction (Article 18) — you may request that we restrict processing of your data in certain circumstances
  • Right to object (Article 21) — you may object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Right to withdraw consent — where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at hello@bamboofutures.co.uk. We will respond within one calendar month. We may ask you to verify your identity before processing your request.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk or by calling 0303 123 1113.

11. Cookies

The Curo website uses a minimal number of cookies:

  • Strictly necessary cookies — required for the website to function. No consent is required for these
  • Analytics cookies — we may use privacy-respecting analytics to understand website usage. These do not track you across other websites

The Curo iOS app does not use cookies.

12. Children's Privacy

Curo is intended for use by UK residents aged 18 and over. We do not knowingly collect personal data from anyone under the age of 18. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, please contact us immediately and we will delete it.
